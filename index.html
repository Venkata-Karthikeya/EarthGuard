🌡️

Climate Change Crisis

Global temperatures have risen by over 1.1°C since pre-industrial times. Extreme weather events — floods, droughts, and wildfires — are becoming more frequent and devastating.

🌊

Ocean Degradation

Over 8 million tonnes of plastic enter our oceans every year. Coral reefs — home to 25% of marine species — are bleaching at unprecedented rates due to warming waters.

🌳

Deforestation

Every minute, the world loses forests the size of 40 football fields. Deforestation drives wildlife extinction, disrupts water cycles, and accelerates carbon emissions.

🌱 Daily Habits

Simple Tips to
Help the Planet

Small changes in everyday life can have a massive collective impact. Start here.

01

Refuse Single-Use Plastics

Carry reusable bags, bottles, and cutlery. Avoid straws and plastic packaging. Even one person refusing single-use plastics prevents hundreds of items from reaching landfills each year.

02

Switch to Renewable Energy

Choose green energy plans from your provider, install solar panels, or use energy-efficient appliances. Reducing dependence on fossil fuels is critical to slowing climate change.

03

Eat Less Meat

Animal agriculture contributes up to 18% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Shifting to a plant-rich diet — even one day a week — significantly reduces your carbon footprint.

04

Use Sustainable Transport

Walk, cycle, or use public transit when possible. Carpool to reduce emissions. Consider an electric vehicle for your next purchase — transport is one of the biggest CO₂ contributors.

05

Compost & Reduce Food Waste

About one-third of all food produced globally is wasted. Composting organic waste returns nutrients to the soil, reduces methane from landfills, and closes the natural loop.

06

Conserve Water Daily

Fix leaking taps, take shorter showers, collect rainwater for plants, and run dishwashers only when full. Fresh water is a finite resource — treat it like the treasure it is.

💡 Bigger Impact

Creative Ideas for
a Greener World

Think beyond yourself — these ideas inspire communities and drive lasting change.

Community

Start a Community Garden

Transform unused land into green growing spaces. Community gardens reduce urban heat islands, promote biodiversity, provide local fresh food, and bring neighbours together around a shared purpose.

Restoration

Organise Tree Planting Drives

A single mature tree can absorb over 20 kg of CO₂ per year. Coordinate with local schools, offices, or municipalities to plant native trees in parks, roadsides, and degraded land areas.

Circular Economy

Upcycle & Repair Instead of Replace

Host repair cafés, upcycling workshops, or clothing swaps in your community. Extending the life of products saves energy, reduces manufacturing pollution, and fosters creative thinking.

Education

Advocate for Green Education

Push for environmental curricula in schools, host awareness campaigns, and support youth-led climate groups. Educating the next generation is the most powerful long-term investment we can make.
